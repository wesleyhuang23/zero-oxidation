What is Oxidation?

Oxidation is a chemical process that occurs in nature where molecules known as free radicals, an atom, molecule or ion with an unpaired valence electron. When these molecules enter the body they begin begin to rob electrons from healthy human cells potentially destroying them or create more free radicals. This process can inhibit our body to perform opitmally and lead verious forms of inflamation and disease. This process happening in our bodies is the same as if iron were to rust. We are rusting as well.

Where do Free Radicals come from?

One way we get free radicals is when our bodies metabolise the food we eat, while breaking them down they create toxins as a by product which can cause free radical damage. The other causes are due to environmental impacts, such as pollution, radiation, durgs, age, injury, infection, stress and poor diet. The most common is from the foods we eat.
